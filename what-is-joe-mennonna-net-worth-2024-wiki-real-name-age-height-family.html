Early Collaborations and Tours

One of the defining moments of Joe Mennonna‘s career came through his work with Ian Gillan and Roger Glover. In 1988, Mennonna contributed his talents to the album Accidentally on Purpose, a project that showcased his ability to seamlessly blend with Gillan and Glover’s artistic visions.

This collaboration was not a mere recording project; it was a testament to Mennonna’s prowess as a musician capable of enhancing the musical journey of established artists.

The partnership with Ian Gillan extended beyond the studio, as Mennonna joined him on tour in September 2006. This tour, in support of the Gillan’s Inn album, allowed audiences to witness Mennonna’s talents live, further solidifying his reputation as a dynamic performer.

Additionally, his appearance on Gillan’s Live in Anaheim offered fans a front-row seat to his musical expertise and versatility.

The Bacon Brothers Band

Mennonna’s musical journey also saw him touring with The Bacon Brothers Band. In this ensemble, he showcased his skills on the Hammond B3 organ, complemented by a 122 Leslie, the Roland Rd-700GX, and the tenor saxophone.

This role demonstrated not just his ability to play instruments but his knack for adding depth and texture to live performances. His tenure with The Bacon Brothers Band exemplified the seamless fusion of technical skill and creative expression, making him a standout musician on any stage.
