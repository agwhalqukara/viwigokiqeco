Jerry Spinelli Writer

At the age of 16, Jerry’s love of sports-inspired him to compose a poem about a recent football victory. His father published it in the local newspaper without his knowledge. While in Gettysburg College, he spent his time writing short stories and was the editor of the college literary magazine, The Mercury.

Jerry became a writer and editor for a department store magazine after his graduation. Jerry’s first few novels were written for adults and were all rejected. His fifth novel was also intended for adults but became his first children’s book. This work, Space Station Seventh Grade, was published in 1982.
