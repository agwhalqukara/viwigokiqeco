Who was Larry Mahan? Know more about him

Larry Mahan is a well-known American former professional rodeo cowboy who was born on November 21, 1943, in Salem, Oregon. Larry is popularly known for being inducted into the ProRodeo Hall of Fame in 1979 in the all-around category.

Larry was just 14 years old  when he started competing in professional rodeo. Starting in 1963, he participated in rodeos with the Rodeo Cowboys Association (RCA). He won the title on five different occasions, between 1966 and 1970, and also in 1973. He won six all-around world championships and two bull riding world championships in the PRCA circuit at the National Finals Rodeo (NFR). The Great American Cowboy won the 1974 Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature for its examination of his 1973 comeback and rivalry with Phil Lyne.
